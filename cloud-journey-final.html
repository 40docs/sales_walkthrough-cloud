THE BOTTOM LINE
They secured the network. But the cloud is bigger than the network.
🔒 Your customer invested in what they know. Firewalls, NGFWs, network segmentation — a mature security posture built over years. That investment is real and it matters.
☁️ But the cloud grew beyond the network. Identities, storage buckets, serverless functions, APIs — none of these touch a firewall. They connect directly to the internet, configured by developers, invisible to the SOC.
🚨 What you can't see, you can't secure. Misconfigurations and over-permissioned identities are the #1 cause of cloud breaches — and they happen silently, every day, outside the firewall's view.
🛡️ CSPM closes the visibility gap. Continuous monitoring across every cloud resource, every configuration, every identity. It doesn't replace the firewall — it covers the 90% of cloud the firewall was never designed to see.
"You locked the front door, but the cloud has windows your alarm can't see."

Help them see what they're missing — before a misconfiguration becomes a breach.

🎯 Discovery Questions By persona ▼
CISO / Security Leadership
How do you currently get visibility into cloud resource configurations — is it manual, scripted, or automated?
Do you have a single view of your security posture across all cloud accounts and providers?
If a storage bucket were misconfigured today, how long would it take your team to discover it?
How are you tracking compliance posture across your cloud estate — continuously, or at audit time?
Cloud / Platform Engineering
How many cloud accounts or subscriptions are your teams operating across?
Who can create new cloud resources — is it centralized, or can any developer spin up services?
How do you manage IAM permissions at scale? Do you have a process for reviewing over-permissioned accounts?
Are your serverless functions and APIs governed by the same security policies as your VMs?
SOC / Security Operations
Can your SOC team see cloud configuration changes in real time, or do they rely on periodic scans?
If an identity was compromised and used cloud API calls to escalate privileges, would your current tooling detect that?
How do your cloud alerts integrate with your existing SIEM and incident response workflows?
Compliance / GRC
Which compliance frameworks apply to your cloud environment — CIS Benchmarks, SOC 2, PCI-DSS, HIPAA?
How are you tracking drift from those benchmarks between audits?
Can you produce a compliance posture report for your cloud estate on demand, or does it require manual effort?
⚔️ Objection Playbook They say / You say ▼
"Our cloud provider handles security."
They secure the infrastructure — the hypervisor, the physical data centers, the network backbone. But under the shared responsibility model, your customer owns how they configure and use those services. A misconfigured storage bucket, an over-permissioned IAM role — that's on them, not the provider. CSPM monitors the customer's side of that responsibility line.
"We already have a cloud firewall."
Cloud NGFW is essential for network traffic between workloads and the internet. But identities, storage buckets, serverless functions, and API endpoints don't route through a firewall — they connect directly via cloud control plane APIs. CSPM isn't competing with the firewall. It covers the resources the firewall was never designed to see.
"We use the cloud provider's native security tools."
Native tools like AWS Security Hub, Azure Defender, and GCP Security Command Center are good starting points. The challenge comes with multi-cloud: if your customer uses more than one provider — or plans to — they need a single pane of glass across all of them. Native tools don't cross provider boundaries. CSPM gives one consistent view regardless of where workloads run.
"We're still early in our cloud migration — this isn't urgent yet."
That's actually the best time to get visibility in place. Misconfigurations tend to accumulate during migration when teams are moving fast and security guardrails haven't been established yet. Starting CSPM early means catching drift before it becomes technical debt — or a breach.
"Our DevOps team reviews configurations in their CI/CD pipeline."
Shift-left is a strong practice — catching misconfigurations before deployment is ideal. But configurations drift after deployment too. Someone changes a security group, a developer updates bucket permissions for testing and forgets to revert. CSPM provides the continuous runtime monitoring that complements what your pipeline catches at deploy time.
"We just passed our compliance audit — we're covered."
Passing an audit is a snapshot in time. Cloud configurations change daily — sometimes hourly. Compliance drift between audits is where risk lives. CSPM provides continuous compliance monitoring against CIS, SOC 2, PCI-DSS, and HIPAA — so your customer knows their posture right now, not just when the auditor was last in the room.
🚀 Next Steps Close the deal ▼
1 Cloud Crisis Room Experience. A hands-on, simulated exercise that walks cross-functional teams through a realistic cloud security incident — such as a misconfigured storage bucket leading to data exposure, or a compromised identity escalating privileges across accounts. Surfaces gaps in cloud incident response and builds organizational readiness.
2 Proof of Value. Connect CSPM to their cloud environment in read-only mode. No changes, no agents — just visibility. Let the findings speak: misconfigurations, over-permissioned identities, compliance gaps, and publicly exposed resources they didn't know about.
3 Executive Briefing. Present what you've learned — from the Crisis Room, the Proof of Value, or both — to CISO/CTO. Lead with their data, not your product. A clear picture of their cloud risk posture is more persuasive than any pitch deck.
